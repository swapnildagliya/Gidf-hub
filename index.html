The Teachers
Colleena Shakti
Rajasthani Folk · Abhinaya · Indian Fusion Bellydance

A specialist in Rajasthani folk dance and the expressive art of Abhinaya, Colleena brings a deep understanding of how the body tells stories — across three workshops at GIDF 2026.

Vaishali Sagar
Bollywood Folk Fusion · Cheraw

Vaishali brings infectious energy and a gift for blending styles — from Bollywood folk fusion to the striking bamboo dance of Mizoram's Cheraw tradition.

Mayurbhanj Chhau · Garba

A master of Mayurbhanj Chhau — the martial folk dance of Odisha — Shampa also brings the celebratory energy of Garba to GIDF, teaching two very different yet equally powerful traditions.

Desihop
Kuthu · Desi-Afro Grooves

Desihop is rooted in Kuthu — Tamil street dance with unstoppable rhythm — and pushes the boundaries further with Desi-Afro fusion, drawing connections across continents through movement.

Manasi
Giddha / Punjabi Folk

Manasi brings the heartbeat of Punjab to GIDF — Giddha, the expressive folk dance of Punjabi women, performed with boliyan (folk verses), clapping, and communal joy.

Swapnil Dagliya
Kalbeliya · Lavani · Festival Curator

Curator of GIDF and artistic director of ABC, Swapnil opens and closes the festival weekend through teaching — leading the Kalbeliya community class on Friday and Lavani on Sunday.

About GIDF

Gent India Dans Festival is an annual celebration of Indian dance, curated by Swapnil Dagliya and organised by ABC at Shoonya Dance Centre in Ghent. GIDF brings together master guest teachers, GIDF company dancers, and audiences to share the extraordinary breadth of India's dance traditions — from the classical to the folk, the ancient to the street.

Frequently Asked Questions
Do I need dance experience to join workshops?

Not at all. Every workshop at GIDF 2026 welcomes complete beginners. Come curious, open, and ready to move — no previous experience needed.

Do I need to book in advance?

Yes — spaces are limited and some workshops are selling fast. Single workshop prices are also higher on the day (€35 vs €30 for 90-min, €45 vs €40 for 120-min), so booking online saves you money too.

What should I wear to workshops?

Comfortable clothing you can move freely in. Dance shoes, socks, or bare feet — no street shoes on studio floors. Bring a water bottle and a small towel.

What should I wear to the Gala Showcase?

Dress to celebrate — smart casual to festive. The Gala is an evening show at Shoonya Theatre. Doors open at 20:00, show begins at 20:30. No photography or video during the performance.

Are workshops taught in English?

Yes, all workshops are conducted in English.

What's the difference between the Opening Party and the Gala Showcase?

The Opening Party (Friday night) is a social celebration — live performances, the Dariya Live Band, and DJ @swapkebolly. The Gala Showcase (Saturday evening) is a seated theatrical performance with 18 acts across ~90 minutes.

Can I attend just one day?

Absolutely. Day passes are available for Friday (€65), Saturday (€110), and Sunday (€110). You can also book individual workshops without a pass.

What is Rise & Riyaaz?

A morning warm-up class with Swapnil to start the day — Saturday 08:45–09:00, Sunday 09:15–09:30. Included with all passes. Not on Friday.
