**Gent India Dans Festival · Edition Four**

## Overview

GIDF 2026 was the fourth edition of Gent India Dans Festival. The landmark of this edition: **live music arrived for the first time** — Hameed Khan Kawa accompanied the Technique & Tools Lab with live tabla, and the Opening Party featured the Dariya Live Band. A new workshop format, the Technique & Tools Lab, was introduced as a deep 4-hour immersive session. Seven master teachers, seven traditions. The Gala Showcase sold out.

## Workshop Descriptions

### Technique & Tools Lab — Colleena Shakti
*Friday 12:00–16:00 · Studio Aakash · With live music by Hameed Khan Kawa · €60 separate*

To embody a folk dance is to imbibe a feeling, a view of life, a sense of humor — the abstract qualities of a culture. This workshop was an exploration of Rajasthani dance techniques from various communities finding their common ground, and the self-expression that expands out from this centre. Reaching beyond mere imitation, students learned choreographic tools to empower their creative range and self-expression. An approach that remains true to Rajasthani roots while building choreography in the lived present.

### Bollywood Folk Fusion — Vaishali Sagar
*Friday 17:00–18:30*

Experience the infectious energy of Mumbai! A powerful, folk-inspired Bollywood routine blending cinematic flair with authentic traditional groundedness.

### Garba Choreography — Shampa Gopikrishna
*Friday 18:30–20:00*

Dive into the circular rhythm of Gujarat. High-energy session focusing on the grace and stamina of Garba, teaching a dynamic choreography that celebrates community, spirit, and footwork.

### Kuthu: The Heartbeat of the Streets — Desihop
*Saturday 09:00–11:00*

The raw, unapologetic energy of Kuthu, the vibrant folk-street dance of Tamil Nadu. Known for its explosive power and earthy footwork — the "rowdy" style translated into a high-octane Desihop choreography. Sweat, loud beats, and pure, unrefined joy.

### The Martial Grace of Mayurbhanj Chhau — Shampa Gopikrishna
*Saturday 11:00–12:30*

One of India's most striking martial art-dance forms, originating from Odisha. Known for stylised animal movements, sweeping leg extensions, and powerful jumps. Fundamental Uli-Chali (basic steps) and the controlled strength required to master this mask-less, heroic dance style.

### Giddha: The Pulse of Punjab — Manasi Khanna
*Saturday 13:30–15:00*

The grace, playful storytelling, and rhythmic boliyan (couplets) of this traditional women's folk dance. Celebrating sisterhood and the vibrant, clapping rhythms of North India.

### Abhinaya: Training Expression for Dance — Colleena Shakti
*Saturday 15:20–16:50*

Indian dances as highly expressive storytelling languages. Traditional ways of developing the eyes and expression from an Odissi classical dance perspective. Conceptual methods of acting based on the Natya Shastra, physical exercises targeting facial responsiveness and movement dynamics, and the study of Odissi dance phrases that demonstrate the tools of abhinaya.

### Indian Fusion Bellydance — Colleena Shakti
*Sunday 09:30–11:00*

Movement vocabulary inspired by cultural dances of India and the Middle East. Bridging modern and folkloric Bellydance/Raqs Sharqi vocabulary with North Indian classical and folk dance postures, Odissi abhinaya, and elements of Central Asian dances. Both Ancient & Modern.

### Desi-Afro Grooves: From Mumbai to Lagos — Desihop
*Sunday 11:00–12:30*

The high-vibration synergy where the rhythms of Africa meet the flavour of India. The "Desi Afro" style — fusing the grounded, polyrhythmic nature of Afrobeat with the infectious energy of Indian street dance. Body isolations, heavy bass-lines, and soul.

### Lavani: The Bold Heart of Maharashtra — Swapnil Dagliya
*Sunday 13:30–15:30*

The theatrical and feminine power of Lavani. Known for its rapid footwork and cheeky storytelling, focusing on the traditional "Dholki" rhythm. Sharp movements and playful Adaa (expressions) that define this vibrant Maharashtrian folk form.

### Bamboo Beats: The Rhythms of Cheraw — Vaishali Sagar
*Sunday 15:50–17:20*

Cheraw, the famous bamboo dance of Mizoram. A masterclass in timing and coordination — dancers navigate the rhythmic clanging of bamboo poles. Intricate patterns and the soaring energy of this unique North-Eastern Indian treasure.
